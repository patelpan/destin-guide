Walking to the Beach

From your door: turn right on Ocean Blvd, then right onto Conch Cay and follow it all the way down to the Private Beach Access and rental store. It's about a 3-4 minute walk.

On the Water & Outdoors

The best adventures on the Gulf and in the harbor, from dolphin cruises and Crab Island to fishing and snorkeling.

Float Crab Island

★ The signature Destin day
★ 4.81,700+ reviews

A shallow, emerald sandbar just inside the pass where boats anchor, music plays, and floating food/drink vendors come to you. Rent a pontoon (with or without a captain) or hop a shuttle. It's the quintessential Destin afternoon. Bring water shoes, a float, and a cooler.

Tip: go on a weekday morning for calmer water and fewer crowds Approx price: shuttle seats from about $39; pontoons commonly $250+ before fees/fuel SunVenture (850) 424-6465 · Crab Island Tours (850) 803-5466

Dolphin Cruise

All ages
★ 4.8–4.9thousands of reviews

Pods of wild dolphins ride alongside the boat almost daily. Southern Star (glass-bottom, two decks) and Destin Dolphin Cruise are top-rated; sunset sailings are magic.

Best for: first-timers, families, romantic evenings Approx price: commonly about $30–$50/person depending on time and vendor Southern Star (850) 837-7741 · Destin Dolphin (850) 200-2260

Season-by-Season

What to expect in Destin all year long, plus our favorite times to visit.

Spring

March to May
  • Water warms from chilly to swimmable by May (~68–78°F)
  • Kite Festival & TurtleFest (April); free Thursday-night harbor concerts begin
  • Excellent inshore & offshore fishing as fish move in
  • Heads-up: March can bring spring-break crowds
Mild, blooming, and far less crowded than summer.

Summer

June to August
  • Peak season: warmest water (~84°F) & every water activity in full swing
  • Big Kahuna's open; July 4th fireworks at the harbor
  • Book charters, cruises, and pontoons well ahead, since they sell out
  • Expect heat, humidity & brief afternoon thunderstorms (and the biggest crowds/prices)
Hot, busy, and the classic full-tilt beach vacation.

Fall

September to November
  • The local sweet spot: warm water lingers into October, crowds thin
  • Destin Seafood Festival (early Oct, free) kicks off the month-long Fishing Rodeo
  • Daily harbor weigh-ins all October, free and fun for kids
  • Shrimp & Grits Festival and arts events round out the season
Arguably the best time to visit: great weather and fewer people.

Winter

December to February
  • Mild & quiet (50s–60s°F); beaches gorgeous but too cool to swim
  • HarborWalk holiday lights, New Year's fireworks & ball drop, boat parade
  • Mardi Gras parade on the island (Feb); 30A Songwriters Festival in Jan
  • Best deals and no restaurant waits. Bundle up for sunset cruises
Calm, festive, and a great-value getaway for couples & snowbirds.

Good to Know

Beach Flag Guide

Beach conditions can change fast, and lifeguards may not be on duty. Always check the day's flag warning before you swim.

GreenCalm water. Swim with normal care.
YellowModerate surf or currents. Use caution.
RedHigh hazard. Strong surf and rip currents.
Double RedWater closed to the public. Stay out.
PurpleDangerous marine life, such as jellyfish.

Check the current Destin beach flag warning before you head out at destinmiramar.com/weather.

Book ahead in season

Charters, dolphin cruises, and pontoons fill up days out from May to August and on October weekends, so reserve early.

Sun is no joke

The reflection off white sand is intense. Reef-safe sunscreen, an umbrella, and water are essentials even on cloudy days.

"Hook & Cook"

Several restaurants, like Brotula's, will cook the fish you catch on a charter for a memorable, money-saving dinner.

Getting around

US-98 is the main strip and backs up in summer. Build in extra time, and consider the bay or Mid-Bay bridge to skip traffic.

Cash for tips

Boat crews, parasail, and pontoon staff work hard for tips, so bring a little cash to thank a great guide.
